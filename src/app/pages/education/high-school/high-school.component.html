High School

Saint-Stanislas Mons (September 2013 - June 2019)



I completed six years of secondary education at Collège Saint-Stanislas in Mons, Belgium, from September 2013 to June 2019. Upon graduation, I was awarded the CESS (Certificat d'Enseignement Secondaire Supérieur), the Belgian diploma for general secondary education.

Throughout my time at Collège Saint-Stanislas, I followed a challenging academic track focused on Advanced Sciences with English Immersion. In this immersion program, all science classes were conducted entirely in English, which not only strengthened my understanding of scientific concepts but also honed my technical English skills, laying a strong foundation for my future studies in an international context.

In addition to sciences, I studied English as a standalone subject in an immersion setting. This environment fostered my fluency and confidence in both spoken and written English as we explored literature, grammar, and composition entirely in English. To further develop my linguistic and analytical skills, I also selected Latin, which provided insights into the roots of many languages, and Spanish, which broadened my linguistic abilities and gave me a foundational grasp of another widely spoken language.

This blend of rigorous scientific study, language immersion, and classical education equipped me with a versatile skill set. My high school years were foundational in shaping my problem-solving abilities, cross-cultural communication skills, and analytical thinking—assets that have greatly benefited my studies and career in computer science.
